1. Checking the official source (JDIH — Jaringan Dokumentasi dan Informasi Hukum, and Lembaran Negara Republik Indonesia)
2. Verifying the provision is currently in force (berlaku) and has not been amended or revoked
3. Reading the full context of cited provisions in the original Indonesian text
4. Consulting case law (yurisprudensi) and academic commentary for interpretation
5. Considering how recent legal developments may affect the matter
6. Checking applicable regional regulations (Peraturan Daerah) where relevant

## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Indonesian legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **Official government databases (JDIH)** | Authoritative | Statute text (primary reference) |
| **Lembaran Negara (State Gazette)** | Authoritative | Promulgated laws and government regulations |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Case law, cross-references (verify independently) |
| **ASEAN treaty databases** | Authoritative for regional law | ASEAN obligations metadata |

**CRITICAL**: Community-maintained resources are **NOT** official government publications. While generally reliable, they may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage. Indonesia's legal system encompasses national law (Undang-Undang), government regulations (Peraturan Pemerintah), presidential regulations (Peraturan Presiden), and regional regulations (Peraturan Daerah) across 38 provinces.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with commercial legal databases** such as:

- Hukumonline, JDIH Premium, LexisNexis Indonesia

These services provide editorial oversight, comprehensive annotations, and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Indonesian law changes continuously through new statutes, government regulations, ministerial regulations, and court decisions
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in the Lembaran Negara
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" (berlaku) may have been:
  - Amended after the last database update
  - Revoked (dicabut) but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional rules affecting its applicability
  - Modified by omnibus legislation (e.g., the Job Creation Law and its derivatives)
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law** -- Coverage is limited; do not rely solely on this for yurisprudensi research
2. **Historical statute versions** -- Limited availability of historical provision wording
3. **Legal commentary** -- No editorial annotations, academic commentary, or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** -- Lower court decisions and administrative rulings largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** -- Limited coverage of parliamentary deliberations (risalah sidang) and academic papers (naskah akademik)
6. **Regional regulations** -- Peraturan Daerah from 38 provinces and hundreds of regencies/cities may not be fully covered
7. **Islamic law sources** -- Compilation of Islamic Law (Kompilasi Hukum Islam) and Religious Court decisions may have limited coverage
8. **Adat (customary) law** -- Traditional customary law recognized in various regions is not included

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without commentary and preparatory works, interpretation may be incorrect
- **Regional Law Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess regional regulation variations across Indonesia's provinces
- **International Law Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess international treaty obligations or ASEAN compliance
